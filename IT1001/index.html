Amazing Features

Google Trips makes world exploring easier with one-stop solution for all essential info, available even offline.
To begin a trip, sync with Gmail or simply search for the destination city and put in the dates.
Tap into a trip and explore the amazing features on a colourful grid of options!

Getting Around / Need to Know

Getting Around: Contain travel information such as public transport, airport transfer, taxi service, driving, walking and biking.

Need to know: Provide useful information about wayfinding, local currency and emergency contacts.

Things to Do

Get categorised activity suggestions based on trip destination, duration and weather, including starred items on Google Map and local travel tips. Tap on "Magic wand" to obtain a random highlight from the city. Retrieve real-time information about operation hours and weather adjustment with internet.

Work Offline

Download each trip to your phone to access information offline. Work well without having to buy extra cellular data or panic when there is no service in some remote location.

Saved Places / Food & Drink

Saved Places: Tap the star icon to add the place you like.

Food & Drink: Provide categorized restaurants located in walking distance along with their ratings, types of cuisine, prices, reviews, locations, opening hours and contact details.

Day Plans

Get popular and customizable day plans organized on a map based on your interests and time, along with reviews and ratings by other travellers. Find a specially customized “For You” list created with what Google knows about you. Tap "Magic wand" to obtain a fresh set of updated nearby attractions.

Reservations

Find all travel reservations automatically gathered from Gmail and organized into individual trips, retrievable offline. See your flight, hotel, car rental and restaurant bookings all in one place.




Reflection & Personal Experience

Everyone likes enjoying a trip, but find it annoying to spend time planning the trip especially when one is busy. Planning a trip for a group is even more difficult and tedious as everyone has different preferences, interests and priorities. Before coming across trip planning app, my travel itineraries, bookings and trip information were scattered across emails, Google Doc and notes. There are too many information sources such as online reviews, recommendations from friends or travel guides. Squeeze everything into a limited time period is not an easy task. In fact, most travellers feel stressful when they struggle to figure out the trip details. Goole Trips, with all the features listed above, seems like a great solution to all these problems.

I am impressed by the amount of information that Google Trips app is able to provide. Previously, I have conducted a user survey in 18 Jan 2017 to collect public opinions about travel planning apps. The survey aims to identify users’ trip planning styles and favourite features. Over 100 responses from respondents across all age groups have shown that most of the users are used to find pieces of information online to plan their trips. As Google is famous for its prevalent search engine, users will be more willing to believe in what Google Trips has to offer. The highly voted favourite features such as offline mode, transportation route, weather consideration, customization, travel tips and information are also provided by Google Trips, which makes it an app with great potential to conquer the growing travel market.

However, the app is still lacking some popular features. A product survey conducted in 8 Jan 2017 shows that many existing travel planning apps provide a more comprehensive feature lists compared to Google Trips. The survey has shown that there are rooms for improvement for Google Trips to catch up with the missing features. Moreover, some of its existing features are not designed in a user friendly manner. Matching personal experience with user reviews in Google Play store, many obvious complaints that point out feature inadequacies can be easily spotted among comments. These user feedbacks serve as a guide for Google Trips to improve and enhance its content to better attract and retain users.

This website hereby provides evaluations about strengths and weaknesses of different features in Google Trips, along with some recommendations. With its strengths of gigantic pool of data, easy-to-use design and familiar interface, Google Trips stands a good chance to be the choice of the travellers.



       


Features that are Great

Google Trips offers more than 200 major cities with complete guides – it is capable of making comprehensive suggestions for interesting activities, important landmarks, transportation and culinary experiences.

  • Available in both Android & IOS platforms
  • Clean & neat grid layout with easy-to-use interface for navigation
  • Provide useful information about things that travellers need to know
  • Offer a list of things to do with considerations of time, place & weather
  • Introduce flexibility in trips by providing real time updates & “Magic wand”
  • Integrate Google Maps to visualize transportation & route information
  • Capable to work offline for easy data retrieval & save on internet charges
  • Bookmark interested places for future visits
  • Suggest customizable itineraries with recommended attractions that suit your timeframe
  • Automatically store your data to propose destinations & activities based on your preferences
  • Import trip reservations from Gmail with a list of upcoming & previously completed trips
  • Free for use & do not require monetary subscription to enjoy all services

Features that can do Better

Despite having substantial wonderful features, Google Trips can do better if it is able to address the following issues.

  • Unable to manually add reservations that are not picked up from Gmail
  • Unable to make reservations directly from the app as it only shows those reservations that are already made
  • Unable to flexibly move reservations from one place to the other
  • Unable to sync from multiple Gmail accounts, such as integrating both work and personal accounts
  • Unable to edit some trip details, such as adding extra days to suggested day trips, including saved places that are not listed in the itinerary or rerouting undesirable trip routes on map
  • May not be adequately equipped for less mainstream destinations as the app mainly focuses on large cities
  • Consume huge storage by downloading offline data
  • Unorganized pile of saved places
  • Unable to add additional information, notes or attachments to trips
  • Privacy concerns about Google collecting your personal data & tracking your location around the world




Recommendations

A list of recommendations is provided below to improve the existing features in Google Trips and suggest additional features that are favourable by the trip lovers.

  • Integrate with transportation & accommodation booking (Google Flights, Expedia, etc)
  • Integrate with Google Search function within the app
  • Integrate with Google Photos to generate travel diary & photo album
  • Integrate with Google Keep for additional notes, lists, photos and audios
  • Enable additional collaborators for group planning (Google Drive concept)
  • Integrate with Google Destinations to include more comprehensive information in travel guides
  • Include more travel tips such as packing list suggestion, safety reminder
  • Integrate with Google Translate for automatic translations & language tips
  • Include social media connection for trip sharing (Google+, Facebook, etc)
  • Allow more manual inputs and trip customization for better flexibility
  • Develop Web app to allow trip planning using browsers
  • Support information retrieval from multiple accounts
  • Include flight tracking capability and real time updates
